# Governance Model

The system is designed for human-supervised augmentation, not autonomous decision-making.

## Core rule

> Agents may assist with research, drafting, monitoring, and implementation. Humans remain responsible for decisions, publication, and irreversible action.

## Approval boundaries

Human approval is required before:

- sending public or official communications;
- posting on social media;
- deploying public-facing changes;
- changing infrastructure, firewall, authentication, or access controls;
- deleting or overwriting important data;
- spending money;
- sharing sensitive information externally.

## Role separation

Role separation reduces error and overreach:

- research agents gather and explain;
- diplomacy agents analyze implications;
- engineering agents implement;
- QA/security agents verify;
- coordinator agents synthesize and ask for approval;
- humans decide.

## Documentation discipline

Important decisions should be recorded with:

- context;
- options considered;
- rationale;
- risks;
- follow-up actions.

## Public communication posture

The public framing should avoid implying that agents conduct diplomacy, replace officials, or make official decisions.

Preferred framing:

> AI agents support research, monitoring, drafting, and operational workflows under human supervision.

Avoid:

> AI agents negotiate, decide, or represent official positions.
